# Thesis Tracker

Maintain a living thesis, not a static opinion. Keep the original idea clear, track what has changed, record both confirming and disconfirming evidence, and end with an updated conviction and action view.

## Workflow

### 1. Define the thesis baseline

If the thesis is new, capture:
- Company name and ticker
- Long / short / watchlist
- One-sentence thesis statement
- 3-5 key pillars supporting the thesis
- 3-5 risks or falsifiers
- Key catalysts
- Valuation or target framework if relevant
- What would make the thesis fail

If the thesis already exists, restate the current baseline before updating it.

### 2. Process the new information

For each update, log:
- date
- what changed
- which thesis pillar it affects
- whether it strengthens, weakens, or does not change the thesis
- whether it changes timing, valuation, or risk

Do not treat every headline as thesis-relevant. Focus on what actually changes the investment case.

### 3. Maintain a scorecard

Keep a running view of the thesis pillars.

| Pillar | Original expectation | Current status | Trend |
|---|---|---|---|
| | | | |

Use simple trend labels such as `improving`, `stable`, `mixed`, `concerning`, or `broken`.

### 4. Track catalysts and watchpoints

Maintain a short catalyst list:
- upcoming earnings
- product launches
- regulatory decisions
- management changes
- guidance resets
- industry datapoints that could validate or break the thesis

If the user wants a meeting-ready format, read `references/output-template.md` before drafting.

### 5. Conclude with conviction and action

End every update with:
- current conviction: high / medium / low
- thesis status: intact / weakening / broken / too early to tell
- action: no change / keep watching / increase / trim / exit / revisit after catalyst

## Missing-data behavior

If the user asks whether a thesis is intact but provides little context, do not stop at asking for more. First outline the thesis review frame, identify the minimum missing facts, and show what would be needed to judge the thesis properly.

## Output standard

Default to a concise thesis review memo with:
1. Current thesis in one sentence
2. What changed
3. Pillar scorecard
4. Key catalysts / watchpoints
5. Updated conviction and action

## Quality bar

- A thesis must be falsifiable.
- Track disconfirming evidence as rigorously as confirming evidence.
- Separate business quality from stock attractiveness when relevant.
- Avoid rewriting history to protect the original thesis.
- If the thesis is broken, say so plainly.
